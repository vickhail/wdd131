How Rankings Are Done

When ranking books I use the follow a little system

One-star reviews: These are for books I couldn't get through. They failed to capture my interest or had elements that made it difficult for me to continue reading.
Two-stars: These books I made it through but wasn't a fan. They were readable, but I probably won't seek out other works by the same author.
Three-stars: These were good and enjoyable, but not great or memorable. They are solid reads that I would recommend to others looking for something pleasant and engaging, but they didn't leave a lasting impact on me.
Four-stars: These books were great reads that I thoroughly enjoyed. They kept me engaged from start to finish and are ones I would eagerly recommend to fellow readers.
Five-stars: These books made me feel or react with emotion, and I remember everything about them. They are memorable, with elements that stay with me long after I've finished reading, and I would definitely recommend them to others.

In this blog, you can also rate the books that you have read, you may use this system or any other you have.

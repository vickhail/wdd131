Chile

Chile is a long, narrow country stretching along the western edge of South America, with 6,435 km (4,000 miles) of coastline along the Pacific Ocean. It is bordered by Peru to the north, Bolivia to the northeast, Argentina to the east, and the Drake Passage in the far south. Chile's landscape features a diverse landscape, including the Atacama Desert in the north, which is one of the driest places on Earth, fertile valleys and vineyards in the central region, and the rugged Andes mountains that run the length of the country. The southern part of Chile is known for its fjords, glaciers, and forests.
